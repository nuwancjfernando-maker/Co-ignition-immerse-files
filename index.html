Brian Eno

The man who made the studio an instrument

He never learned to play. He invented a way of working instead — and it reshaped popular music, software, and how we think about making anything.

  • Invented ambient music — Music for Airports (1978)
  • Produced Bowie, Talking Heads, U2, Devo, Coldplay
  • Coined the term "generative music"
  • Created the Oblique Strategies (1975) to break creative deadlock
  • Composed the Windows 95 startup sound — six seconds, heard a billion times

If creativity were a gift, you could only admire it. Eno proves it's a system — which means you can learn it. Give it four minutes.
